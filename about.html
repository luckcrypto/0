Under the hood

What our RNG actually does

A lot of websites generate numbers with Math.random() — convenient, but not designed to be unpredictable, and in some cases reproducible. Our generators use crypto.getRandomValues, the browser's cryptographically-secure randomness, the same class of randomness trusted for encryption keys and security tokens.

We also do it correctly: instead of a naïve "remainder" trick that subtly favours some numbers, we use rejection sampling so every number in a range is equally likely — no hidden bias. Picks are made on your device and never sent anywhere. The result is randomness that's fair by construction, not by promise.

Important and worth repeating: a generator only produces random combinations. It can't predict a draw or improve your odds — nothing can. It simply gives you a fair, unbiased pick.

SHA-256 · powered by you

Randomness you create with your own hand

For some games we go a step further with our SHA-256 entropy generators. Instead of the machine deciding everything, you become the source of randomness: you move your mouse or finger, and we capture hundreds of unpredictable positions and micro-timings. That motion is fed through SHA-256 — the very same hash that secures Bitcoin — to produce a one-time seed, which then drives the number selection.

Why bother? Because it makes each set of numbers genuinely yours and effectively impossible to reproduce or foresee — a beautiful blend of human chaos and cryptographic rigour. To be crystal clear, it doesn't change your odds one bit; every combination is still equally likely. It's about trust, transparency, and a bit of delight — not an edge.

Crypto × chance

Why crypto and lotteries belong together

It might look like we wear two hats — lotteries and cryptocurrency — but they share the same DNA: cryptography and verifiable randomness. The SHA-256 hash behind our entropy generators is the same primitive that secures Bitcoin. The thing that makes a blockchain trustworthy — being able to prove something is fair rather than asking you to take it on faith — is exactly what a lottery should offer too.

That's the connection we're excited about: using crypto-grade techniques to make games of chance provably fair and transparent, for a community that's global and borderless by nature. Our crypto tools and market data are there to inform and explore — never financial advice — while the deeper idea is philosophical: chance done openly, with maths anyone can check.

Honesty about odds, money & wellbeing

Lotteries are games of pure chance. No generator, pattern, or system — ours included — can predict a draw or improve your odds. Every possible combination always has exactly the same probability of being drawn. We will never claim otherwise.
